Hi everyone,

Over the last few months, we’ve been talking to builders, grant curators, and teams across the Polkadot ecosystem. Conversations ranged from hackathons and PBA cohorts to treasury bounties and long-running infrastructure projects.

The same theme kept resurfacing in different forms:

> it’s not always clear where a motivated builder should go to find their first (or next) paid opportunity in Polkadot.

Polkadot clearly doesn’t lack ambition or funding mechanisms. What it might lack is a smoother “last mile” between **people who want to contribute** and **the opportunities that need them**.

Before we talk about anything we’re building, we want to sanity-check whether we’re even describing this **problem** correctly.

---

## What We Think Is Going On

In recent years, Polkadot has invested heavily into **developer support and funding**:

- treasury bounties for open-source development, UX and infrastructure  
- programs like the Open Source Developer Grants  
- hackathons, educational initiatives, and ecosystem campaigns  

At the same time, we still hear from builders who:

- aren’t sure where to look for their *first* grant or bounty  
- only discover opportunities by accident (a random tweet, a DM, a buried Discord message)  
- feel that opportunities are spread across too many places to keep track of  

Our working hypothesis:

> Polkadot has strong funding and programs,  
> but we don’t yet have a great **“talent & opportunity discoverability layer”** for the average builder or small team.

Below is how this looks from both sides.

---

## From a Builder’s Point of View

For a new or returning builder, the opportunity landscape can look like this:

- treasury bounties on Subsquare / Polkassembly  
- programs like Open Source Developer Grants, UX/infra bounties, Fast Grants  
- hackathons, competitions, and PBA-related opportunities  
- ad-hoc bounties and RFPs on X / Twitter, Discord, Matrix, Telegram  

Each of these has its own page, docs, and channels.

If you already know the ecosystem well, you can piece it together. But if you’ve just finished PBA, a course, or your first side project, it’s easy to feel:

> “I’m sure there’s something I could work on… but I don’t know where to look, and I’m probably missing half of what exists.”

Other pain points we hear:

- It’s hard to tell which opportunities are suitable for a **solo dev vs a team**, or **junior vs senior**.  
- You can’t just go to one place and see “what’s out there for someone like me right now”.

---

## From a Curator / Team Point of View

On the other side, if you’re running a **treasury bounty, grant program, or team budget**, a different set of issues shows up:

- **Launch → spike → fade**  
  - big effort into designing the bounty  
  - initial announcement on forum / X / Discord  
  - a short wave of attention, then visibility drops unless someone actively keeps re-sharing

- **Multi-channel overhead**  
  - questions and applications arrive via forum, Discord, Telegram, email, DMs  
  - a lot of time goes into just keeping the process coherent across tools

- **Reaching the “right” builders is hit-or-miss**  
  - it’s not obvious how to consistently reach builders with the right skills and interest  
  - there may be great fits who simply never see the opportunity in time

The result: there is genuine funding and intent to support builders, but the **matching problem** (who should see which opportunities, and when) is still more ad-hoc than it probably needs to be.
