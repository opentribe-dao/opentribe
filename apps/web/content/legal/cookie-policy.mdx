## What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

**How Cookies Work:**
- Stored by your web browser on your device
- Sent back to the website on each subsequent visit
- Allow the website to recognize your device
- Remember your preferences and actions

**Types of Cookies by Lifespan:**
- **Session Cookies:** Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser
- **Persistent Cookies:** Remain on your device until they expire or you delete them

**Types of Cookies by Origin:**
- **First-Party Cookies:** Set by Opentribe directly
- **Third-Party Cookies:** Set by external services we use (analytics, authentication providers)

## Why We Use Cookies

1. **Essential Functionality:** Authentication, security, and core platform features
2. **User Experience:** Remember your preferences (theme, language)

4. **Security:** Protect against fraud, spam, and abuse
5. **Performance:** Monitor and improve site performance

### Cookie Categories Explained

#### 1. Strictly Necessary Cookies (No Consent Required)

These cookies are essential for the platform to function properly. You cannot disable them as they are necessary for core functionality.

**Cookies:**
- `better-auth.session-token` - User authentication
- `csrf-token` - Security protection
- `cookie-consent` - Your cookie preferences

**What Happens If You Block These:**
- You cannot log in or use authenticated features
- Security protections may not work
- Your cookie preferences won't be saved

#### 2. Functional Cookies (Consent Required)

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization. The platform will work without them, but your preferences won't be remembered.

**Cookies:**
- `theme` - Dark/light mode preference
- `locale` - Language preference (English, etc.)

**What Happens If You Block These:**
- Theme preference resets on each visit
- Language preference resets on each visit
- You'll need to manually set preferences each time

#### 3. Analytics Cookies (Consent Required)

These cookies help us understand how users interact with our platform so we can improve it.

**Google Analytics Cookies:**
- `_ga` - Main analytics identifier
- `_gid` - Secondary identifier
- `_gat` - Request throttling

**PostHog Cookies:**
- `ph_*` - Feature usage tracking
- `ph_phc_*` - Session tracking

**Data Collected:**
- Pages viewed and time spent
- Navigation paths and click patterns
- Geographic location (country/city level)
- Device type, browser, screen resolution
- Referral source

**What Happens If You Block These:**
- Your usage data won't be collected
- You won't contribute to platform improvement insights
- All core functionality remains available

## How to Manage Cookies

You have several options to control and manage cookies:

### 1. Cookie Consent Banner (Recommended)

When you first visit Opentribe, you'll see a cookie consent banner where you can:
- Accept all cookies
- Reject non-essential cookies
- Customize your preferences

You can change your preferences at any time by clicking **"Cookie Settings"** in the footer of any page.

### 2. Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Here's how for popular browsers:

**Google Chrome:**
1. Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
2. Choose "Block third-party cookies" or "Block all cookies"

**Firefox:**
1. Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
2. Choose "Delete cookies and site data when Firefox is closed"

**Safari:**
1. Preferences → Privacy
2. Check "Block all cookies"

**Microsoft Edge:**
1. Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data
2. Choose "Block third-party cookies" or "Block all cookies"

**Note:** Blocking all cookies may prevent you from using essential platform features like logging in.

### 3. Analytics Opt-Out Tools

**Google Analytics Opt-Out:**
Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on:
https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

**Global Privacy Control (GPC):**
Enable GPC in your browser to automatically opt-out of data sharing:
- Supported in browsers like Firefox, Brave, DuckDuckGo

### 4. Mobile Device Settings

**iOS (Safari):**
Settings → Safari → Block All Cookies

**Android (Chrome):**
Chrome → Settings → Site settings → Cookies → Block third-party cookies
